Relations in Prolog

Prolog programs specify relationships among objects. When we say, "John owns the book", we are declaring the ownership relationship between two objects: John and the book. When we ask, "Does John own the book?" we are trying to find out about a relationship.

Relationships can also be described by rules such as the following, 

        Two people are sisters if
                are both female and
                they have the same parents.
This is a rule which allows us to find out about a relationship even if the relationship isn't explicitly stated as a fact.

Programming in Prolog consists of:

  • declaring some facts about object and their relationships;
  • defining rules about objects and their relationships;
  • asking questions about objects and their relationships.
